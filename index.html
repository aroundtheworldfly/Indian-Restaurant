The Business Plan
Mission Statement

Our idea is to spread the authentic, delicious taste of India, to people who seek to try Indian Cuisine. Indian Food is one of the most popular culinary art globally, with dishes varying from vegetarian to non vegetarian, allowing inclusion of people with variable dietary requirements and preferences. Our restaurant aims to rectify problems for people with different/special dietary requirements, such as for vegetarians, and to also provide an atmosphere of inclusion for foreigners and localities. Our target market will be generally among the Southeast Asian community, as there is a high presence of nationalities from the subcontinent. We will also aim for adventurous localities, who want to try different kinds of foods, ranging from extremely spicy to mild kinds. We are aware that Poland, especially Warsaw, is an area of multiculturalism, home to myriad ethnics and using that to our advantage will generate large amounts of profits for the business. Moreover, with the growing diversity in food, we want to ensure everyone’s dietary needs are fulfilled hence we will be catering to food allergies and intolerances (e.g. dairy free), special dietary requirements (e.g. vegetarian) and religious reasons (e.g. halal). This line of business would not only be beneficiary in terms of bringing in revenue but is also aligned with our passion for cooking. We will be the sole owners of The Indian Kitchen. Our restaurant will be inclusive, traditional and the best in the city providing unbelievable customer service and delicious food. Apart from in house service and alcohol, deliveries will also be available. Five years from now, we hope to expand to other areas in Warsaw and Poland as a whole. As there are multiple Indian restaurants in Warsaw, we acknowledge that there will be fierce competition, especially in the Center. Hence, we will take great care to be at par with our competitors, and how to springboard ourselves to new heights.

Business Structure

Although our business might not be as big as some of the leading brands we will ensure we put the right structure in place that will support the kind of business growth we have in mind. We will hire qualified and honest people to occupy the following postions:

  • Restaurant manager- managing the daily activities in the restaurant ( kitchen included)
  • Chef\kitchen staff- in charge of preparation of cuisines
  • Accountant\cashier- responsible for financial transactions, reports, budgets and statements for the business
  • Waiters/waitress- attends to customers in a friendly and professional manner
The restaurant will have the following ownership structure: Both the owners will hold a 40 percent stake each and our investor will hold a 20 percent stake (the investor is later mentioned in the Sources of Finance and Pricing Strategies)

Sources of Finance and Pricing Strategies

Finance is the most integral part of the business. We have found an investor, who is prepared to give us 100 thousand zloty as a one time payment, and is also ready to pay three months rent. We will keep the cost of main dishes between 15-25 zloty, starters hovering around 10 zloty, and drinks in the range of 3 to 7 zloty. As the venture becomes more profitable over time, we will gradually decrease the prices. Our prices will be one of our Unique Selling Points, as our competitors have higher prices for food. We have also have our investor on board to pay, in full, the furniture costs.

Sources of Food/Ingredients

We have decided to buy rice, flour, and Indian spices from a food manufacturer directly. We will receive amounts of rice, flour and spices twice a week, and we have agreed on a price, which is to be paid on a monthly basis. We have also decided to buy sustainable packaging materials from another manufacturer directly, which will help us be environmentally-friendly and also help us operate/pickup services.

Target Market

Our market is crucial for us to understand as to what customers should we primarily focus on. We will focus on many traits and qualities. We will as mentioned, of course primarily focus on adventurous localities and the Southeast Asian Diaspora in the Warsaw Metropolitan area, but will also expand our focal lenses on people who have different dietary requirements, work employees and students. Furthermore, in our current world, a business cannot succeed without the aid of technology, hence we will establish an online presence, such as on Social Media. Adding on, we understand the acute need of paying heed to the environment, so we will also strive to making our business as green as possible

Our Menu

As our menu will be widely inclusive we will serve a wide range of dishes that will include only the best quality of ingredients. All of the ingredients used and their nutritional value will be posted on our blogs for our customers to understand. In addition our packaging will consist of reusable materials such as corrugated cardboard as it is regarded as environmentally friendly. Moreover, we acknowledge that there are many popular dishes in the Indian cuisine, which is why we will include them. Examples are Curry (Vegetarian or Non-Vegetarian), Chicken dishes, and Breads.

Market Research

To aid our efforts in understanding our customers, we gave out an electronic form, and anyone could fill it. This helped us in understanding the market on a broader scale. We found out that all of our respondents had Indian food at least once in the past two months, 21 percent of people had Indian food twice in the past two months, 5 percent of people had Indian food thrice in the past two months, 2 percent of people had Indian food four times in the past two months and 18 percent of people had Indian food more than 6 times in the past two months. This gave us a correlation that people had Indian food on average, at least twice in the past two months.

We then also asked our participants that what are their favorite dishes in the Indian Culinary arts. 53 percent of people answered Curry, 34 percent of people answered Chicken dishes, and 36 percent of people answered Breads. This gave us valuable information; we now know on what dishes to primarily focus on, and to make them of best quality.

Moving further, we asked our participants if they prefer cost over quality, quality over cost or a balance. We found out that 53 percent of people prefer a balance, 45 percent of people prefer quality over cost, and 2 percent prefer cost over quality. This gave us a correlation, that we can make our dishes of best quality, and also make them less expensive, (compared to our competitors) so that customers can buy more at the same time, and enjoy equally. We also found out that 63 percent of people prefer good quality tables and chairs, which supports our correlation to make dishes less expensive, and people can enjoy more, and also experience proper levels of comfort. 37% of people prefer not going further than 5 kilometers for an Indian restaurant, and 32 percent of people are willing to go any distance. This shows that customers will be ready to go for any lengths to have Indian food, which is why we will make it a point to invest properly in aesthetics and features such as being kid friendly (a mini playground zone, or prams) and good quality furniture, and traditional paintings over walls. By doing these small things, we can expect lots of customers, and we could perhaps become a household name.

Marketing (Promotions)

We, as in the owners, have decided, to open up an Instagram channel of our restaurant, as we cannot look away from the Power of Social Media. We will use Instagram as our e-marketing and promotions platform, which will help us catapult further and attract new customers. We, the owners, will run and maintain the channel.

Product and services

All products have been mentioned above in the menu

As we are new and this is a startup, we will not intially operate delivery services, but will operate takeout immediately. When our financials will be stable, we will consider in operating a delivery service.

Strengths and Weaknesses

A major weakness that will be held up against us is the fact that we are a new Indian restaurant, and we are new in the game. We in the beginning do not have neither the financial capacity, nor the manpower or resources to compete against other well established Indian restaurants. Moreover, as we lack experience in operating a restaurant business in the long term, we will be needing trusted advisors to guide us on our path to success. Our competitors to name a few are Mr India, Bombaj Massala and India King, who are well established within the Southeast Asian diaspora.

Another weakness, as aforementioned, is that we are new within the Indian Restaurants business. So the beginning will pose some difficult times, but we sincerely hope that if our startup is effective, we will become a household name.

Our main strength is that of our price (and location), as other Indian restaurants are selling food at high costs. This will be our main aim and one of our unique selling points to attract customers. Our other USP is that we will have cooks from the Indian subcontinent. This will help our products be favored and preferred.

What’s more, is that since an Indian is a co-owner of the restaurant, this will aid in taking the venture to new goals, as he will know how to adapt the restaurant to the regular Southeast Asian’s needs.

Location

As aforementioned in Market Research, we have seen that 37 percent of people prefer to not travel more than 5 kilometers, and 32 percent of people are willing to travel any distance. At the same time, we need to be weary of our finances, as we are a startup, and cannot risk it. Hence, we have decided to keep our location in Mokotow, where rents are affordable whilst we follow the results of our Market Research.

We will have our restaurant at Postępu 6, which is an office complex. The rent of the place is 8065 zloty per month, with a service charge of 2574 zloty. Another extra strength is that of air conditioning, ease of parking (as this is a complex), elevator for the immobile, and a reception desk. Moreover, it is located near to Galeria Mokotow, and accessible to the major highways, which makes our restaurant freely accessible.
